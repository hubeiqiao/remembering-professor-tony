# CIMO framework and client engagement strategy for digital transformation research

Fri, 15 Aug 25

### Issues

- Startups vs SMBs audience differences
  - Two distinct audience types requiring different approaches
  - Startups lack legacy systems and established processes
  - SMBs have existing infrastructure, history, and resistance to change
  - Need template with different “flavors” based on audience
- Abstraction level challenges
  - Must operate at all levels: high-level and detailed
  - Companies want specifics, not just high-level concepts
  - Risk of being too abstract without actionable details
  - Need to go “really deep” with evidence and implementation specifics
- Tools, methods, frameworks, theories integration
  - Too many tools (100+ identified) will overwhelm clients
  - Need to standardize on core tools, keep others as supplements
  - Must avoid showing up with 13+ different tools
- Process and leadership templates vs customization
  - Templates needed but must be customizable
  - Balance between standardization and client-specific needs
  - Risk of being “too niche” if over-customized
- Adding words vs clarifying
  - Clarifying means better words and more detail, not more pages
  - One sentence explanations need detailed breakdown at operational level

### CIMO compliant staged-process

- Framework integration missing
  - Seven frameworks not incorporated into current process
  - Need to identify where framework/theory/model selection occurs in Stage 1
  - This represents core competitive advantage
- Must-do revisions required
  - Process steps need to be defined by outputs, not activities
  - 20 total outputs across three stages
  - Activities vs outputs distinction critical for academic credibility
- Version 1 readiness assessment
  - Current process ready for initial application
  - Perfection not required - learn through doing
  - Apply 10 times to identify needed refinements
- Real vs simulated clients
  - Real clients preferred for authentic experience
  - ChatGPT5 can simulate client responses when needed
  - Historical precedent: paid retired professionals to simulate clients

### Process steps defined by their outputs & organized by who leads

- Client requirement absolute
  - Without client, project becomes “garbage”
  - Real problems requiring research essential
  - Must solve something new, not replicate existing solutions
- Output focus mandatory
  - Steps defined by deliverables, not activities
  - Concrete, specific, confined deliverables required
  - Industry focuses on what you deliver, not what you do
- Leadership dynamics change by stage
  - Stage 1: Team leads most activities, presents to client
  - Stage 2: Client leads, team provides co-creation support
  - Gate 1: Team responsible for presentation
  - Gate 2: Client responsible with team support
- Co-creation strategy
  - Act as “Sherpa” guiding clients through process
  - Softly nudge and navigate relationships
  - Cannot dictate - must facilitate client ownership
