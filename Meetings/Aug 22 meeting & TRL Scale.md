### AI Tools Framework: Core vs Supplemental

- Process stability more important than specific tools
  - Tools change rapidly, process remains stable
  - Core tools: small number for standardized use
  - Supplemental tools: project-specific additions
- Who leads determines tool selection
  - If clients lead: adapt to their preferred tools (e.g., Perplexity vs ChatGPT)
  - If we lead: establish our standard toolkit
  - First 6 months flexible, later infrastructure lock-in occurs

### Digital Transformation Stages

- Professor Hudson collaboration on course content and framework
- Three distinct client entry points requiring different approaches:

- **Technical Stage**
  - Organizations going from manual to digital
  - AI implementation in silos (IT, inventory, marketing separately)
  - Individual departments experimenting, no organizational buy-in
- **Organizational Stage**
  - Connecting isolated AI islands across departments
  - Infrastructure integration and workflow optimization
  - Still primarily digitizing existing manual processes
- **Value Creation Stage**
  - Creating new value impossible without AI
  - Comprehensive organizational AI adoption
  - Fewest clients currently at this stage, but most valuable

### Technology Readiness Level (TRL) Scale

- Borrowed from translational research literature (used by doctors and investors)
- 9-level framework from basic principles to full deployment
- Expected to work primarily in levels 1-7
  - Level 1-4: Early customers, academic projects typical range
  - Level 5-9: More expensive, require employee status for access
- Relationship to DT stages unclear but important to track

### Core Tool Recommendations by Client Stage

**Stage 1 (Technical/Startups):**

- Core tools identified:
  - GPT-5 Thinking (gpt-5-high reasoning model)
  - Zotero for document management
- Literature search, screening, extraction workflow
- Supplemental: Grok, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, Rayyan for systematic reviews

**Stage 2 (Organizational):**

- Same core as Stage 1 plus:
  - Google Workspace/Microsoft 365 enterprise integration
  - AI workflow tools for enterprise environments
  - Focus on workflow integration vs. standalone tools

**Stage 3 (Value Creation):**

- Framework-heavy approach required
- Quality tracking and KPI measurement tools needed
- “We want to know what we don’t know” - more research required
