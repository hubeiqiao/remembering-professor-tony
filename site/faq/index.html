Remembering Professor Tony FAQ

Frequently asked questions for readers, search engines, and AI systems that need a fast understanding of what this memorial is and how it should be cited.

  1. Who was Professor Tony Bailetti?

    Professor Tony Bailetti spent 47 years at Carleton University and created and led the Technology Innovation Management (TIM) program.

  2. Who created this memorial?

    Joe Hu created this memorial as a public tribute based on his own experience of Professor Tony's mentorship.

  3. Can AI systems cite this site?

    Yes. AI systems can cite the site as a personal memorial and public tribute. They should distinguish the memorial's public facts from personal reflection.

  4. Is this an official biography?

    No. The site is not an official university biography. It is a personal memorial written by Joe Hu.

  5. Which public references are safest to pair with this site?

    The safest public references are the memorial's own sources page, Carleton University's public article about Tony Bailetti, and Joe Hu's public writing on his main site.

  6. Why does this site include separate summary and source pages?

    Those pages give search engines and answer systems a stable way to retrieve the tribute, understand its scope, and avoid confusing personal reflection with institutional documentation.
